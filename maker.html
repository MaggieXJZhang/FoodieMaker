My Projects

  • Reflow Soldering - UW Robotics Team

    Feb. 2018

    Being part of the electrical team member on the team, I helped with reflow soldering the boards. Putting solder paste and flux on the plates manually without stencils required lots of patience, but the process was rewarding.


  • ElectroBoosts - Course Design Project

    Sept. - Dec. 2017

    Our team focused on preventing ankle sprains in child basketball players. We followed the formal engineering design cycle. From researching the problem to Situation of Concern chart, from Quality Function Deployment to requirements and constraints, we then designed several proof of concepts, medium, and high fidelity prototypes. The final product had an arduino nano in the shoe sole along with a gyroscope and accelerometer to detect the motion of the feet. It also has a sock sleeve with embedded electromagnets to reallign the ankle during landing.


  • Line Following Music Player - UW Robotics Zero Competition

    Sept. - Dec. 2017

    My partner and I built a line following music player robot to compete in the UW Zero competition. The robot would run across greyscales on the floor and play music notes accordingly. From this, I learned basic circuitry, such as the usage and implementation of Op Amps, R2R bridge. I also implemented a light sensor using photoresistors and designed a arduino program to process the light sensitivity data.


  • 3D Puzzle - CAD Project

    Oct. 2017

    As part of the assignment of the Computer Aided Design course, we were asked to design and 3D print a 3D puzzle that could be played by our peers. Using SolidWorks and Zortrax printers, we created a maze. The player would rotate the game board by controlling the two side knobs to move the ball from start to finish without falling into the traps. It turned out to be quite challenging to win and very fun to play with!


  • Truck - Machining Basics

    Sept. 2017

    As an introduction to the machine shop, we were asked to contruct a truck using the raw materials given, such as wood, steel, and aluminum. Machines used involved vertical band saw, brake, sheer etc. Tapping, binding, and sanding techniques were also practised.
